What is an Acid?

An acid is a chemical that contains hydrogen ions and is typically found in liquid form. When acids interact with bases, they donate these hydrogen ions to the reaction, which frequently results in the production of salts. Certain acids are corrosive because they are highly reactive and can burn or dissolve other substances upon contact. When an acid interacts with a pH-sensitive item, such as litmus paper, it turns red, signifying a low pH level, and that a substance is acidic.

What is a Base?

A base is a substance that can accept hydrogen ions in water. Through chemical reactions, bases can neutralize acids, producing salt and water as byproducts. Many alkaline substances tend to feel soapy or slippery on the skin when touched. Additionally, bases can change the color of some pH-sensitive items, such as litmus paper, to blue, signifying a higher pH, indicating that the substance is basic.
